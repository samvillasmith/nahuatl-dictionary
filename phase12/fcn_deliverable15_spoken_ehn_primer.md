**Project:** Flor y Canto Nahuatl

**Status:** Internal/public draft scaffold  
**Scope:** Beginner through early conversational spoken EHN

---

## 1. Purpose

This book is the beginner-facing spoken entry point into Flor y Canto Nahuatl.

It is designed to:
- teach spoken EHN first,
- build conversation before literary elevation,
- keep primer material free of hidden classical contamination,
- and provide a clean path from survival phrases to routine communication.

This primer is built on the project’s instructional track:
- 23 primer units
- 13 A1 units
- 6 A2 units
- 4 B1 bridge units
- contamination status: clean

---

## 2. Pedagogical Principles

1. **Speech first**
   The primer teaches spoken EHN as the foundational learner path.

2. **No hidden classical contamination**
   Classical material is not silently used as ordinary spoken content.

3. **Construction-based teaching**
   Learners are taught reusable patterns, not just isolated words.

4. **Dialogue-first progression**
   Each unit begins from meaningful speech, not abstract grammar.

5. **Review is mandatory**
   Vocabulary, constructions, and dialogues recur through review cycles.

## 5. Dialogue Policy

Dialogues must be:
- primer-safe
- conversation-oriented
- high-frequency
- built from spoken-safe vocabulary and constructions

Dialogues must not:
- rely on poetic diction
- rely on classical-only forms
- blur spoken EHN with MSN poetic style
- hide constructed or normalized status when relevant

---

## 6. Drills

Required drill types:
- substitution drills
- response drills
- transformation drills
- question-answer drills
- sentence building
- short roleplay prompts

---

## 7. Grammar Notes

Grammar notes must be:
- short
- functional
- construction-linked
- learner-readable

They should explain:
- what the pattern does
- how to use it
- what not to confuse it with

They should avoid:
- unnecessary philological detours
- literary/classical density
- register confusion

---

## 8. Review Sections

Each unit review should include:
- vocabulary recall
- construction recall
- dialogue recall
- listening or reading recognition if available
- speaking prompt
- writing prompt
- mastery checklist

---

## 9. Glossary

The glossary should be:
- unit-linked
- searchable
- English and Spanish friendly
- marked for spoken use where necessary

Fields should include:
- EHN form
- gloss_en
- gloss_es
- lesson number
- notes
- caution flag if not default spoken-safe

---

## 10. Contamination Rule

All primer material must be screened for:
- classical-only items
- ceremonial-only items
- poetic-high items disguised as ordinary speech

If such material appears, it must be:
- removed,
- reclassified,
- or explicitly marked as non-primer material.
