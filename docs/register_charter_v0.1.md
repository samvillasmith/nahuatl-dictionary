# Flor y Canto Nahuatl Register Charter

**Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl for Speech, Modern Standard Nahuatl for Writing**

Project: Flor y Canto Nahuatl
Abbreviations: FCN, EHN, MSN, MNS-N, MSN-P
Version: 0.1
Date: March 25, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This Register Charter establishes the official register system of Flor y Canto Nahuatl. Its purpose is to define the language layers used by the project, govern how forms are labeled, prevent silent mixing of incompatible varieties or styles, and make possible a coherent system for teaching, writing, publication, and literary creation.

The register system exists to ensure that FCN can support all of the following without confusion:

- spoken instruction
- neutral written communication
- public prose
- literary writing
- songs and poetry
- dictionaries and grammar
- source-based comparison with historical and non-EHN Nahuatl material

## 2. Governing Principle

Flor y Canto Nahuatl is built on register clarity.

A form must never be treated as self-explanatory simply because it appears in a source. Every lexical item, sentence, text, annotation, and publication in FCN must belong to a defined register or carry a clearly stated status.

No register may silently impersonate another.

In particular:

- spoken EHN must not be overwritten by literary/classical diction without explanation
- poetic/classical diction must not be passed off as everyday spoken language
- historical citation forms must not be treated as neutral public prose unless explicitly adopted into MSN
- comparative forms must not be treated as FCN-standard forms unless explicitly normalized and approved

## 3. The FCN Register System

FCN recognizes the following official registers and statuses.

### 3.1 EHN Colloquial

**Tag:** `EHN_colloquial`

This is the core spoken register of Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl as used for everyday speech, beginner instruction, basic dialogue, oral comprehension, and practical language learning.

**This register is the default for:**

- beginner lessons
- dialogues
- conversation practice
- oral drills
- pronunciation models
- everyday examples

**This register should prioritize:**

- natural spoken forms
- teachable everyday syntax
- high-frequency vocabulary
- communicative clarity
- minimal literary elevation

**This register must not be burdened with:**

- unexplained archaisms
- ornamental classical diction
- artificially raised syntax
- poetic repetition treated as ordinary speech

### 3.2 EHN Formal

**Tag:** `EHN_formal`

This is a more careful or elevated spoken EHN register used for respectful speech, public speaking grounded in EHN, instructional address, or prepared oral delivery that remains recognizably spoken EHN.

**This register may appear in:**

- formal oral presentations
- speeches
- careful public-facing recordings
- upper-level spoken materials
- certain ceremonial or reflective contexts when still grounded in EHN

It differs from EHN_colloquial by degree of formality, not by becoming literary-classical.

### 3.3 MNS-N

**Tag:** `MSN_neutral`

This is the neutral written standard of Modern Standard Nahuatl. It is the primary written register of FCN for clear, disciplined, publicly usable prose.

**This register is the default for:**

- orthographic norms
- dictionaries
- grammar explanations
- educational texts
- public essays
- subtitles
- captions
- general publication
- non-poetic literary prose intended for broad readability

**This register should prioritize:**

- orthographic stability
- lexical transparency
- cross-text consistency
- readability
- moderate formality
- compatibility with teaching and publication

This register is not colloquial speech written down without mediation, and it is not poetic elevation for its own sake. It is the standard reference layer.

### 3.4 MSN Public

**Tag:** `MSN_public`

This is a formal public-facing extension of MNS-N used for statements, manifestos, institutional writing, public cultural documents, ceremonial prose, and other contexts requiring dignity and clarity without full poetic elevation.

This register may draw on slightly higher diction than MSN_neutral, but it must remain readable and stable.

**This register may be used for:**

- project statements
- educational institutions
- public letters
- dedications
- commemorative texts
- formal subtitles or narration
- speeches prepared for publication

It must not become indistinguishable from MSN_poetic.

### 3.5 MSN-P

**Tag:** `MSN_poetic`

This is the elevated literary register of FCN. It is the official high register for poetry, songs, lyrical prose, rhetorical performance, ceremonial expression, and consciously artistic writing.

**This register may include:**

- elevated diction
- parallelism
- refrain structures
- poetic compression
- vocatives
- ceremonial turns of phrase
- selected classical resonance
- deliberate intensification of tone and image

**This register is appropriate for:**

- lyrics
- poetry
- artistic recitation
- literary prose
- ceremonial writing
- reflective and sacred rhetoric

This register must remain governed. Not every classical-looking line is good poetic Nahuatl, and not every elevated form belongs here automatically.

### 3.6 Classical Citation

**Tag:** `Classical_citation`

This status is used for forms cited from Classical Nahuatl or historical lexical sources that are being preserved for reference, comparison, philology, source tracking, or literary inheritance.

A form marked Classical_citation is not automatically part of FCN active usage.

**It may appear in:**

- lexicon notes
- comparative notes
- etymological discussions
- literary commentary
- source appendices
- poetic drafting materials under review

It may not appear as neutral FCN prose or as beginner spoken material unless it has been explicitly adopted into another register.

### 3.7 Comparative Only

**Tag:** `Comparative_only`

This status is used for forms from non-EHN Nahuatl varieties or cross-dialect comparative materials included for lexical comparison, contrast, or documentation.

**These forms may appear in:**

- comparative tables
- dialect notes
- source appendices
- research materials
- editorial deliberation

They are not FCN-standard forms unless explicitly normalized and adopted.

### 3.8 Proposed

**Tag:** `Proposed`

This status is used for editorially proposed forms, normalizations, coinages, reconciliations, or stylistic solutions that are not yet sufficiently validated to be treated as stable FCN forms.

**A proposed form may be:**

- a modern standardized headword
- a normalized spelling
- a newly regularized public prose form
- a literary modernization based on source evidence
- a coinage for modern usage

Every proposed form must carry rationale.

### 3.9 Deprecated

**Tag:** `Deprecated`

This status is used for forms previously considered but now discouraged for FCN use because they are misleading, unstable, poorly supported, overly artificial, or superseded by a better solution.

Deprecated forms remain documented, but they should not be propagated.

### 3.10 Needs Review

**Tag:** `Needs_review`

This status is used for forms whose source, reading, register, gloss, orthography, or function remains uncertain.

No Needs_review form should appear in core public materials without explicit note.

## 4. Register Hierarchy

When FCN needs to determine the default register for a form or text, the hierarchy is:

1. **EHN_colloquial** for spoken teaching and ordinary dialogue
2. **EHN_formal** for elevated but still spoken EHN
3. **MSN_neutral** for general writing
4. **MSN_public** for formal public prose
5. **MSN_poetic** for literary and lyrical elevation
6. **Classical_citation** for historical reference
7. **Comparative_only** for dialect comparison
8. **Proposed** for editorial candidates
9. **Deprecated** for dispreferred items
10. **Needs_review** for unresolved items

This is not a prestige ladder. It is a functional system.

## 5. Domain Assignment Rules

### 5.1 Beginner Lessons

**Allowed by default:**

- EHN_colloquial

**Allowed with care:**

- EHN_formal
- limited MSN_neutral in notes only

**Not allowed without explicit marking:**

- MSN_public
- MSN_poetic
- Classical_citation
- Comparative_only

### 5.2 Intermediate and Advanced Language Lessons

**Allowed:**

- EHN_colloquial
- EHN_formal
- MSN_neutral

**Allowed with explicit labeling:**

- MSN_public
- MSN_poetic
- Classical_citation

### 5.3 Dictionaries

**Allowed:**

- all official registers and statuses

**Required:**

- every entry must display register/status clearly
- classical and comparative material must be visibly distinguished from active FCN standard usage

### 5.4 Grammar Manuals

**Allowed:**

- all official registers and statuses

**Required:**

- examples must be labeled by register
- pedagogical examples should prefer EHN or MNS-N unless the chapter is specifically literary

### 5.5 Public Prose

**Default:**

- MSN_neutral

**Allowed:**

- MSN_public

**Allowed sparingly and intentionally:**

- MSN_poetic

**Not allowed without note:**

- Classical_citation
- Comparative_only

### 5.6 Songs, Poems, and Literary Writing

**Default:**

- MSN_poetic

**Allowed:**

- MSN_neutral
- MSN_public
- selective adapted material informed by Classical_citation

**Required:**

- publication annotations should identify where diction is literary, historical, or deliberately elevated when relevant to comprehension

### 5.7 Ceremonial or Sacred Language

**Default:**

- MSN_public or MSN_poetic, depending on tone

**Allowed:**

- carefully controlled classical resonance

**Required:**

- no false implication that all ceremonial expression is identical with historical Classical Nahuatl

### 5.8 Subtitles and Captions

**Default:**

- MSN_neutral

**Allowed:**

- MSN_public in formal contexts

**Not preferred:**

- heavy MSN_poetic, unless the source material is itself poetic

## 6. Register Conversion Principles

### 6.1 EHN to MNS-N

When moving from spoken EHN to neutral written MSN, FCN may:

- regularize orthography
- clarify ambiguous forms
- choose a standardized headword
- stabilize morphology for writing

FCN may not:

- silently replace everyday spoken vocabulary with elevated literary diction
- inject classical forms merely to sound prestigious

### 6.2 MNS-N to MSN-P

When moving from neutral writing to poetic writing, FCN may:

- elevate diction
- compress syntax
- introduce parallelism
- intensify imagery
- use refrain structures
- adapt classical resonances deliberately

FCN may not:

- obscure meaning beyond reasonable literary purpose
- treat random archaism as poetic quality

### 6.3 Classical Citation to Active FCN Usage

A classical form may move from Classical_citation into MSN_poetic, MSN_public, or exceptionally MSN_neutral only if:

- its meaning is sufficiently understood
- its orthography is normalized
- its function is clear
- its adoption is editorially justified
- its register placement is explicit

## 7. Silent Mixing Is Prohibited

The following are prohibited:

- presenting poetic lines as if they were ordinary EHN conversation
- presenting classical citation forms as if they were neutral FCN prose without notice
- blending comparative dialect forms into FCN standards without declaration
- changing register mid-text without purpose or annotation
- hiding editorial proposals inside apparently "traditional" forms

Every text must know what it is.

## 8. Labeling Requirements

**Every lexical entry in FCN must include:**

- a register or status tag
- a source reference or editorial basis
- a validation status where applicable

**Every example sentence must include:**

- its register
- translation or gloss when pedagogically needed

**Every published text should include:**

- its dominant register
- notes for significant departures or elevated diction when appropriate

## 9. Publication Rules

A form may be published in core FCN materials only if one of the following is true:

- it is source-supported and appropriately registered
- it is speaker-validated and appropriately registered
- it is an explicit editorial proposal and labeled as such

A form may not be published as neutral standard usage if it is only:

- historically attested
- aesthetically appealing
- inferred without support
- borrowed from another dialect without declaration
- carried over from OCR noise or ambiguous parsing

## 10. Register Approval Rules

### 10.1 Approval into EHN

A form may be approved into EHN only if:

- it is attested or validated as compatible with spoken Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl
- it functions naturally in speech
- it does not depend on literary explanation to be usable

### 10.2 Approval into MNS-N

A form may be approved into neutral written MSN only if:

- it is orthographically stable
- semantically clear
- editorially consistent
- suitable for readable prose

### 10.3 Approval into MSN-P

A form may be approved into poetic MSN if:

- it is aesthetically coherent
- functionally literary
- intelligible enough for its intended audience
- not merely an unexamined antique import

## 11. Review Standards

Each form should eventually receive review under the following questions:

- What is its source?
- What register does it belong to?
- Is it active, comparative, historical, or proposed?
- Can it be taught safely?
- Can it be published safely?
- Does it preserve the distinction between speech and literary elevation?
- Does it strengthen or confuse FCN?

## 12. Default Register Rules

If no register is specified:

- spoken lesson material defaults to **EHN_colloquial**
- grammar and dictionary prose defaults to **MSN_neutral**
- public statements default to **MSN_public**
- poetry and lyrics default to **MSN_poetic**

No classical or comparative form may ever be assumed by default.

## 13. Final Declaration

The FCN register system exists to protect clarity, dignity, and continuity. It gives spoken Nahuatl, written Nahuatl, and poetic Nahuatl each a rightful place without collapsing them into one another. By distinguishing registers openly, FCN seeks to make Nahuatl easier to learn, easier to publish, and stronger as a living language of both daily use and artistic greatness.
