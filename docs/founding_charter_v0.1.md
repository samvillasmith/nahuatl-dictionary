# Flor y Canto Nahuatl Founding Charter

**Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl for Speech, Modern Standard Nahuatl for Writing**

Version: 0.1
Date: March 25, 2026

---

## 1. Name and Identity

Flor y Canto Nahuatl is a linguistic, pedagogical, and literary project dedicated to establishing a coherent modern framework for Nahuatl speech, writing, teaching, and artistic creation. Its public subtitle is *Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl for Speech, Modern Standard Nahuatl for Writing*.

Internally, the project uses the following shorthand:

- **FCN** = Flor y Canto Nahuatl
- **EHN** = Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl
- **MSN** = Modern Standard Nahuatl
- **MNS-N** = Modern Standard Nahuatl, neutral written register
- **MSN-P** = Modern Standard Nahuatl, poetic/classical written register

## 2. Mission

Flor y Canto Nahuatl exists to establish Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl as a teachable spoken standard, Modern Standard Nahuatl as a respected written norm, and a living poetic high register that reconnects Nahuatl speech, literature, song, and public life.

## 3. Purpose

FCN exists to build the missing infrastructure that allows Nahuatl to function in a modern, coherent, and culturally serious way across multiple domains. Its purpose is to support:

- everyday speech and instruction
- writing and publication
- lexicography and grammar
- literature and songwriting
- education and public communication
- long-term continuity between living speech and historical literary inheritance

FCN is not only a teaching effort, and it is not only an archival effort. It is a standardization project, a pedagogical project, a literary project, and a cultural-linguistic revival project.

## 4. Foundational Structure

Flor y Canto Nahuatl is built on three connected but distinct layers:

### A. Spoken Layer: EHN

Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl serves as the spoken base of the project. It is the register of speech, pronunciation, basic instruction, everyday dialogue, and practical communication.

### B. Written Layer: MNS-N

Modern Standard Nahuatl, neutral register, serves as the project's written norm. It is the register of orthography, headwords, dictionaries, educational texts, essays, public prose, captions, subtitles, and general written communication.

### C. Literary Layer: MSN-P

Modern Standard Nahuatl, poetic/classical register, serves as the project's elevated literary register. It is the register of songs, poetry, rhetoric, ceremonial language, lyrical composition, and other high forms of artistic or formal expression.

## 5. Core Claims

Flor y Canto Nahuatl makes the following claims:

- Nahuatl requires a clear and disciplined pathway from speech to writing to literature.
- A modern Nahuatl framework should be rooted in a living spoken variety.
- A written norm is necessary for teaching, publication, lexical stability, and public use.
- A poetic high register is necessary if Nahuatl is to live fully in song, literature, and elevated expression.
- A serious standard must distinguish clearly between spoken, neutral written, and poetic/classical forms.

## 6. What FCN Does Not Claim

Flor y Canto Nahuatl does not claim:

- to replace all living Nahuatl varieties
- to erase local or community speech traditions
- to declare Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl the only legitimate spoken Nahuatl
- to equate Classical Nahuatl with modern spoken EHN
- to treat all historical forms as immediately suitable for present-day everyday speech
- to present editorial normalizations as if they were unmediated inherited facts

FCN is a framework for continuity and use, not a denial of diversity.

## 7. Intended Audiences

Flor y Canto Nahuatl is intended for:

- learners seeking a teachable spoken Nahuatl
- teachers developing structured Nahuatl materials
- writers seeking a stable written norm
- lyricists and poets composing in a dignified high register
- translators and editors working across registers
- scholars interested in modern standardization and literary development
- readers and creators who want Nahuatl to function as a living modern language

## 8. Respect for Living Nahuatl Communities

FCN is founded in respect toward living Nahuatl speakers and communities. It does not exist to override community knowledge, but to support continuity, clarity, and broader usability. Living speech is not raw material to be discarded in favor of literary prestige; it is the foundation on which FCN is built.

Where FCN normalizes, it will do so transparently. Where FCN compares, it will do so honestly. Where FCN proposes, it will label those proposals clearly.

## 9. Register Discipline

FCN requires strict distinction between registers.

- EHN must remain identifiable as spoken Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl.
- MNS-N must remain identifiable as neutral written Modern Standard Nahuatl.
- MSN-P must remain identifiable as poetic/classical elevated Nahuatl.

No text may silently drift between these layers without explanation. No poetic/classical form may enter beginner spoken materials without being clearly marked as literary or elevated. No classical diction may silently overwrite living spoken EHN by default.

## 10. Provenance and Editorial Transparency

FCN is committed to source traceability and editorial transparency.

Every major lexical, orthographic, grammatical, or stylistic decision must be traceable to one or more of the following:

- source evidence
- speaker validation
- explicit editorial proposal

Nothing should be presented as settled tradition if it is actually the result of modern normalization. Every normalization should be reviewable, and whenever possible reversible to the source form or rationale from which it was derived.

## 11. Publication Goals

FCN intends to produce, in time, a coherent set of public materials, including:

- a founding charter
- a register charter
- a source hierarchy
- a master lexicon
- an orthography manual
- a style manual
- a spoken EHN primer
- a Modern Standard Nahuatl manual
- a poetic/literary Nahuatl manual
- a dictionary
- an annotated corpus
- lyric and literary works demonstrating the standard in use

## 12. Final Declaration

Flor y Canto Nahuatl exists because Nahuatl deserves more than fragmentation between archive, classroom, and song. It deserves a living pathway through speech, writing, and creation. FCN therefore commits itself to building a modern Nahuatl framework that is disciplined without being sterile, literary without being detached, and respectful of living speech without abandoning the ambition of public and artistic greatness.
