# Flor y Canto Nahuatl — Core Premises

**Internal Reference Document**

Version: 0.2
Date: March 25, 2026

---

## 0.2.1 Spoken Standard

Flor y Canto Nahuatl adopts Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl as its spoken standard for instruction, dialogue, pronunciation, basic grammar, and everyday communicative use.

## 0.2.2 Written Standard

Flor y Canto Nahuatl adopts Modern Standard Nahuatl as its written standard for orthography, headwords, dictionaries, public prose, educational materials, editorial consistency, and long-form publication.

## 0.2.3 Literary High Register

Flor y Canto Nahuatl recognizes a poetic/classical high register, labeled MSN-P, for songs, poetry, ceremonial rhetoric, literary prose, and other elevated forms of expression.

## 0.2.4 Non-Erasure Principle

Flor y Canto Nahuatl does not claim to replace all living Nahuatl varieties, nor to dissolve community-based speech traditions into a single universal spoken norm.

## 0.2.5 Non-Collapse Principle

Flor y Canto Nahuatl does not equate Eastern Huasteca Nahuatl with Classical Nahuatl, and it does not permit classical diction to overwrite living spoken EHN by default.

## 0.2.6 Three-Layer Principle

Flor y Canto Nahuatl is built on three distinct but connected layers:

- **EHN** for speech
- **MNS-N** for neutral writing
- **MSN-P** for poetic/classical elevated writing

## 0.2.7 Register-Label Principle

Every lexical item, example sentence, text, lyric, and lesson in FCN must carry an explicit register label or be derivable from one.

## 0.2.8 Beginner-Protection Principle

No poetic/classical form may enter beginner EHN teaching materials without being clearly marked and explained as literary or elevated language.

## 0.2.9 Provenance Principle

Every published form in FCN must be traceable either to source evidence, speaker validation, or an explicit editorial proposal. Nothing is to be presented as inherited fact if it is actually a normalization decision.

## 0.2.10 Reversibility Principle

Every normalization in FCN should be reversible to its source form, source note, or editorial rationale so that the project remains transparent, reviewable, and improvable.

## 0.2.11 Respect Principle

Flor y Canto Nahuatl exists in respect toward living Nahuatl speakers, historical Nahuatl literature, and future Nahuatl creators; it is not an extraction project but a framework for continuity, dignity, and use.
