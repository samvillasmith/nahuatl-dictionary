# Flor y Canto Nahuatl — Success Criteria

**Internal Reference Document**

Version: 0.3
Date: March 25, 2026

---

## 0.3.1 What Counts as FCN v0.1

FCN v0.1 is achieved when the project has a stable constitutional and editorial foundation.

**Required for v0.1:**

- project name and subtitle locked
- shorthand locked
- founding charter complete
- register charter complete
- source hierarchy complete
- orthography decision draft complete
- master lexicon schema complete
- editorial rules for provenance, register, and uncertainty complete
- first imported EHN lexical base underway or sampled
- public mission and website language complete

**What v0.1 proves:**

The project is real, coherent, and governable.

## 0.3.2 What Counts as FCN v0.5

FCN v0.5 is achieved when the project has functional linguistic infrastructure and enough curated material to support serious internal development and controlled public demonstration.

**Required for v0.5:**

- core EHN lexicon of at least 500 items curated
- initial classical/reference lexicon linked to the master database
- orthography manual complete
- style manual complete
- grammar blueprint complete
- register conversion guide complete
- first 25–50 annotated example sets complete
- QA protocol complete
- validation framework complete
- internal master sourcebook in working draft
- first sample lesson materials drafted
- first sample literary annotations drafted

**What v0.5 proves:**

The project is not only well-conceived; it is operational.

## 0.3.3 What Counts as FCN v1.0

FCN v1.0 is achieved when Flor y Canto Nahuatl can be published as a coherent, teachable, writable, and culturally serious standard package.

**Required for v1.0:**

- internal master sourcebook complete
- spoken EHN primer complete
- MNS-N / MSN manual complete
- MSN-P / poetic manual complete
- stable curated lexicon in publication-ready form
- validation status visible across core materials
- consistent orthography and register application across all books
- sample dictionary or dictionary prototype complete
- starter corpus complete
- public-facing release packet complete
- versioning and editorial process stable

**What v1.0 proves:**

Nahuatl now has a usable modern framework in FCN for speech, writing, and literary creation.

## 0.3.4 Success Metrics

### A. Linguistic Coherence

FCN is linguistically coherent if:

- EHN, MNS-N, and MSN-P are clearly distinguishable
- no major internal contradictions exist between lexicon, grammar, and style manuals
- the same word is not treated inconsistently across books without explanation
- orthography is stable
- register shifts are intentional and labeled
- normalization rules are consistently applied

### B. Pedagogical Usability

FCN is pedagogically usable if:

- a beginner can progress through spoken EHN without being buried in uncontrolled classical material
- vocabulary is staged by frequency and teachability
- lessons use repeatable grammar patterns
- examples are clear and reusable
- the relation between spoken EHN and written MSN is understandable rather than confusing
- the learner can produce simple correct sentences early

### C. Literary Dignity

FCN has literary dignity if:

- the poetic/classical register feels deliberate rather than ornamental
- elevated diction is coherent and not random archaism
- songs, poems, and literary lines can be annotated systematically
- lyric language can be raised above neutral prose without becoming unintelligible
- the project supports genuine creative work, not just didactic examples

### D. Public Readability

FCN has public readability if:

- neutral written prose can be read without specialist training
- spelling is stable enough for reuse in captions, subtitles, essays, and educational text
- public-facing writing does not depend on unexplained scholarly conventions
- readers can move from sentence to sentence without uncertainty about register or form

### E. Dialect Respect

FCN respects dialects if:

- EHN is treated as the spoken base rather than a token source
- non-EHN varieties are documented without being erased
- the standard does not falsely claim universal spoken authority
- comparative material is labeled honestly
- community speech is not silently corrected into artificial norms

### F. Source Traceability

FCN has source traceability if:

- every major lexical or orthographic decision can be traced to evidence or editorial proposal
- uncertainty is visible
- raw source forms are preserved somewhere in the system
- normalized forms can be compared back to source forms
- published materials do not hide where decisions came from
